Water for River Environment

Definition: Water for the Environment- water left in the river for use by riparian habitats and the animals that inhabit them.

Real World Example: As a society we know that riparian habitats require a certain amount of water flow to sustain the plants and animals that live there. The amount of water needed greatly depends on the time of year, and the particular plants and animals living in the riverine ecosystem. These ecosystems differ depending on factors such as elevation and sun exposure, etc.

Implementation:

  • 0 = no water is intentionally left for the riparian habitats,
  • 100 = the maximum allowable amount of water is left in the river for the environment (15% of the daily or the annual river flow). This means that there will be proportionally less water for municipal, commercial, and industrial water uses.
